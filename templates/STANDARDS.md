## Seeded defaults (author: keep, edit, or delete)

1. **No emotion labeling.** Never state what a character feels as a report
   ("she felt uneasy"). Show it through action, gesture, silence, or the body.
2. **No contrast-negations.** No "not X, but Y" constructions. Write the positive
   thing directly.
3. **No narratorial intrusion.** Don't frame perception as reporting ("he noticed,"
   "she realized"). Fold it into the scene.
4. **Em dashes: avoid.** Default to period or comma. The author inserts one
   deliberately if wanted; the assistant never does.
5. **Sensory before stakes.** At least 2-3 senses (sound, smell, texture,
   temperature) before any fight or high-stakes scene escalates.
6. **False peaks in action.** A moment of apparent control or resolution before the
   situation worsens.
7. **Name the subject on dramatic beats.** No vague "he"/"she" during intense
   scenes; ambiguity kills the beat.
8. **Violence is sudden and physical.** No build-up telegraphing, no extended
   internal reaction. The body moves first; meaning comes after.
9. **Mood words banned in prose:** "grim", "dread", "ominous", "wrong"/"wrongness"
   as vague modifiers. Name the specific deviation instead.
