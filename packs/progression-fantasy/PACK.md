# Genre Pack — Progression Fantasy

Data, not code. This pack adjusts planning and review defaults when NOVEL.md
declares the genre. It never overrides VOICE.md or STANDARDS.md.

## The reader promise

Measurable growth. The reader is here to watch a character become capable through
effort, and to FEEL each increment. Breaking this promise (long stretches with no
growth, or growth that arrives unearned) is the genre's cardinal sin. Secondary
promises: a power system with knowable rules, and payoff for attention (readers
track the system closely; reward them for it).

## Pacing norms

- Some visible progress every arc; a felt increment (new ability, rank, insight,
  resource) at least every handful of chapters. The increment can be small; it
  must be concrete.
- Alternate advancement pressure: training/preparation chapters need a live threat
  or deadline under them, or they read as downtime.
- Power-ups land harder after a demonstrated ceiling: show the character failing
  against something specific before they pass it.
- The gap between earning a capability and USING it should be short early in the
  story and may stretch later (setup debt is a luxury of earned trust).

## System clarity rules

- The reader must be able to predict what is hard for the protagonist. If the
  rules shift to serve the plot, the genre contract breaks.
- New rules are planted before they matter, never invented in the scene that needs
  them.
- Costs make systems real: every capability should have a price (time, resource,
  risk, social standing) the story actually charges.

## Trope ledger

Play straight or subvert deliberately; never drift into one unawares.

| Trope | Watch for |
|---|---|
| Tournament arc | Earns its length only if standings change relationships, not just rankings |
| Hidden master / mentor | Their knowledge must cost something or they become a faucet |
| Bloodline / chosen reveal | Undercuts the effort-promise; if used, make effort still the differentiator |
| Face-slap beat | Satisfying in moderation; a diet of it flattens antagonists into props |
| Rank/level systems | Numbers are a proxy; the reader must feel the difference in scene behavior |

## Failure modes (review flags)

- **Power creep:** stakes inflate until early-story drama looks quaint. Anchor
  stakes to people and costs, not magnitudes.
- **Plateau drag:** chapters of grinding with no narrative consequence.
- **Spectator protagonist:** allies or systems solve what the protagonist should.
- **Unfelt increments:** advancement announced (numbers up) but never demonstrated
  in scene.
- **Rules amnesia:** an established cost or limit quietly skipped under pressure.
