# Ch 1 - The Old Sea-Dog at the Admiral Benbow

**Logline:** A scarred old seaman takes lodging at the Hawkins family inn, hauls a
sea chest upstairs, and pays Jim fourpence a month to watch for a seafaring man
with one leg.

## Events (in order)
1. The captain arrives at the Admiral Benbow: tall, heavy, tarry pigtail, soiled
   blue coat, a sabre cut across one cheek; a sea chest follows him on a barrow.
2. He pays three or four gold pieces for lodging and stays for months without
   paying more; Jim's father never dares demand the rest.
3. He hires Jim for a silver fourpenny a month to keep watch for "a seafaring man
   with one leg" and report the moment he appears.
4. Evenings: rum and water, sea songs ("Fifteen men on the dead man's chest"),
   stories of hanging and walking the plank that terrify and thrill the locals.
5. Dr. Livesey, visiting Jim's ailing father, crosses the captain: the captain
   threatens him with a knife; Livesey faces him down and warns he'll be hunted
   down if he keeps the knife out, and tells him rum will kill him.
